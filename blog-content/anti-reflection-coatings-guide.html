What Are Anti-Reflection Coatings?

Anti-reflection (AR) coatings are thin-film coatings applied to optical surfaces to reduce light reflection and increase transmission. Without AR coating, each uncoated glass surface reflects approximately 4% of visible light, meaning an uncoated lens with two surfaces loses about 8% of incident light. In multi-element systems, these losses compound dramatically.

How AR Coatings Work

AR coatings work on the principle of destructive interference. By carefully controlling the thickness and refractive index of thin film layers, reflected waves from different interfaces cancel each other out, while transmitted waves constructively add together. The result is significantly reduced reflection at specific wavelengths.

Types of AR Coatings

Single-Layer MgF2

The simplest AR coating consists of a single layer of magnesium fluoride (MgF2). It provides modest reflection reduction (R < 2% per surface at design wavelength) over a broad spectral range. This is the most economical option and works well for general-purpose applications.

Broadband Multi-Layer AR

Multi-layer AR coatings use 2-4 thin film layers to achieve very low reflectance (R < 0.5% per surface) over a wider wavelength range. Common broadband options include:

  • BBAR Visible (400-700nm) - General-purpose visible applications
  • BBAR UV (250-400nm) - UV imaging and measurement
  • BBAR NIR (650-1050nm) - Near-infrared applications
  • BBAR SWIR (900-1700nm) - Short-wave infrared

Laser-Line V-Coats

V-coats are designed for minimum reflectance at a single specific wavelength, achieving R < 0.1% per surface. They are ideal for laser applications where every bit of transmission matters. Common wavelengths include 355nm, 532nm, 633nm, 780nm, 808nm, 1064nm, and 1550nm.

Choosing the Right AR Coating

Consider these factors when selecting an AR coating:

  1. Wavelength range - Match the coating to your operating wavelength(s)
  2. Number of surfaces - More surfaces benefit more from better coatings
  3. Laser power - High-power lasers need high-damage-threshold coatings
  4. Environment - Humidity and temperature cycling affect coating durability
  5. Budget - MgF2 is most economical; V-coats cost more but perform best at design wavelength
